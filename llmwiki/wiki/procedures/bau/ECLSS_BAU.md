# Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS)
## Business As Usual (BAU) Procedures

**Version**: 0.2

### Overview
The ECLSS is the most critical subsystem in the habitat. Its primary function is to maintain a survivable environment for the crew through continuous closed-loop recycling of air and water, and active thermal control.

### Nominal Operating Parameters
- **Atmospheric Pressure**: 101.3 kPa (1 atm)
- **Oxygen (O2) Concentration**: 21%
- **Carbon Dioxide (CO2) Concentration**: < 0.4%
- **Water Recycling Efficiency**: > 95%
- **Ambient Temperature**: 21°C - 24°C

### Subsystems & Operations
1. **Air Revitalization System (ARS)**:
   - Continuously scrubs CO2 from the atmosphere.
   - Generates O2 via electrolysis of water when concentration drops below 20.5%.
   - **Constraint**: Requires stable power. If power drops, O2 generation must be paused before CO2 scrubbing.

2. **Water Recovery System (WRS)**:
   - Processes humidity condensate and urine into potable water.
   - **Constraint**: Halting the WRS for more than 48 hours will deplete reserve potable water tanks.

3. **Ventilation & Thermal Control**:
   - Ensures uniform air mixing to prevent localized CO2 pockets.
   - Disperses heat generated by crew and electronics.

### Emergency Intersections
- If Power_Grid_Management reports a `CRITICAL_LOW` battery state, ECLSS may temporarily degrade temperature control and halt O2 generation (relying on reserves) to save power, but CO2 scrubbing **MUST NEVER BE HALTED**.
- In the event of a pathogen outbreak (see Medical_Quarantine_Procedure), ECLSS must isolate ventilation sectors. This drastically increases power consumption due to independent filtering and disrupted thermal flow.
